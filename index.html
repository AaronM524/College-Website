WELCOME TO

THE UNIVERSITY OF CALIFORNIA, LOS ANGELES

Los Angeles, California


UCLA, or the University of California, Los Angeles, has a distinctive blue and gold logo. It is located in Los Angeles, California. You can contact the university at (310) 825-4321. UCLA's mission is to provide a high-quality education and to promote research and innovation. The school values diversity, inclusivity, and community engagement, aiming to create a supportive environment where students can learn, grow, and make a positive impact in the world.


STUDENT LIFE





Campus Facilities and Amenities:

UCLA offers a variety of facilities, including modern classrooms, libraries, research centers, and sports complexes. Students have access to dining halls, coffee shops, and study spaces throughout the campus, making it easy to find places to relax or study.

Student Organizations and Clubs:

There are over 1,000 student organizations at UCLA, covering a wide range of interests, from academic and professional groups to cultural clubs and hobby-focused organizations.

Social and Extracurricular Activities:

UCLA hosts numerous social events and activities throughout the year, including concerts, movie nights, and cultural festivals.

Housing Options and Cost:

UCLA offers various housing options, including on-campus dorms, apartments, and family housing. Costs can vary based on the type of accommodation and meal plans, but living on campus typically ranges from moderate to high. Many students find that living on campus enhances their college experience by keeping them close to classes and activities.

Tuition & Fees: UCLAâ€™s tuition and costs are designed to be manageable, with most families paying less than the full price. For the 2024 academic year, California residents pay approximately $34,667, which includes university fees, housing, and other expenses. Non-residents face additional costs, totaling around $63,669 due to supplemental tuition.

Financial Aid Options & Payment Deadlines: UCLA offers various financial aid plans to help students cover the costs of tuition and other expenses. These include scholarships, grants, loans, and work-study opportunities. To access financial aid, students must complete the FAFSA or CA Dream Act application by the April 2 deadline. The BruinPay Plan allows students to break their tuition payments into three smaller installments per term, making it easier to manage costs. Additionally, UCLA provides support for parents with options like low-interest loans and college savings accounts. Financial aid helps make a UCLA education affordable for many families.

ALUMNI SUCCESS


Career Outcomes and Job Placement Rates:
UCLA provides great career support through its Career Center, helping students find jobs and connect with employers. The university has a 93% graduation rate over six years for first-year and transfer students, showing its focus on helping students succeed. Graduates go on to work for top companies like Google, Amazon, and Disney, which shows how valuable a UCLA education is in the job market.

Industry Partnerships and Internships:
UCLA works with top companies in different fields to offer students internships and real-world experience. Through these partnerships and the Handshake platform, students can join job fairs, workshops, and networking events. This practical approach helps students make important connections and learn skills that employers want.

Curriculum Details

UCLA's academic programs mix theory and real-world skills. Each major has specific courses, including required classes, optional ones, and final projects. Students learn by doing hands-on work in labs, research, and group projects, which helps them get ready for their future jobs.

Faculty Profiles

UCLA's Curriculum Management System (CMS) helps faculty create, revise, and manage courses and academic programs. Each major has specific courses students must take, including core classes, electives, and final projects. Faculty can track the progress of course approvals and access information about current majors, minors, and past course offerings.
